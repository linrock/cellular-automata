Cellular automata

Cellular automata are grids of "cells" where each cell changes over time based on the state of its neighbors.

Elementary cellular automata

The simplest automata are one-dimensional rows. Each cell has two possible states. The state of a cell in the next generation depends only on its current state and the state of its two neighbors.

We can visualize the evolution of a one-dimensional automata over time by adding new generations to the bottom. We'll then see a pattern that appears to scroll upwards.

Conway's Game of Life

The most popular two-dimensional cellular automaton was created by John Conway in 1970. In each new generation, the state of the world is generated based on these rules:

  • A dead cell with three neighbors becomes a live cell
  • A live cell with two or three neighbors stays alive
  • All other live cells become dead cells
